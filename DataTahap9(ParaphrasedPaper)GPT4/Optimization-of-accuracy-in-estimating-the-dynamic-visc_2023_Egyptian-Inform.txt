Wang et al. (2021) provide an extensive examination of the role of nanofluids in enhancing heat pipe systems, showcasing the synergy between machine learning techniques and their applications in this domain. Their review offers theoretical insights, practical applications, and predictions concerning this innovative integration.

Jamei et al. (2021) explore thermal conductivity in oil-based hybrid nanofluids by combining an Extended Kalman Filter with a feed-forward neural network. This robust approach aims to improve the accuracy of thermal conductivity assessments in these advanced materials.

Wang et al. (2020) introduce artificial neural network (ANN) models developed specifically for predicting the thermal conductivity of hybrid nanofluids in waste heat systems. Their models establish a framework for anticipating the thermal performance of these fluids, potentially optimizing the utilization of waste heat resources.
