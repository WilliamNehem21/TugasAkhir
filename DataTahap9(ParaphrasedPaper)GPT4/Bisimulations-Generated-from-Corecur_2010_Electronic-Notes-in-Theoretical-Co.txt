As for the recursive process described, it involves an iterative method where the element tk+1 is produced from tk. This process includes two main simplification steps that act upon the head and tail of tk. Part of the simplification allows for the substitution of any recursive variable x's head with a new object parameter, labeled dx, and the tail with a new recursive variable named x'. This simplification implies that one can hypothetically consider x as composed of the new head, dx, followed by the new recursive variable, x'.
