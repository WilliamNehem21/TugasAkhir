Prior to finalizing the spin model for the read-write lock (rwlock), it became evident that the implementation had flaws. Merely going through a verification review proved enlightening. Specifically, when applying for a read lock (rdlock), a reader is required to wait if there is already a writer in line (indicating writer preference). During this wait, the counter for queued readers increases. However, the counter issue was that it wasn't being reduced thereafter. Red Hat recognized this defect and addressed it in the following version of their software.
