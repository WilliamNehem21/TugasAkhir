The input unit provides the main unit with information about the domain, which in this case pertains to the human body and environmental conditions. These data are derived from physiological sensors that are connected to the main unit. These sensors may include a variety of types. For example, electrodiagnostic studies aim to identify any issues along the peripheral nervous system pathway and pinpoint the specific location of the problem.

This study employs a basic expert healthcare system unit. A new function, such as the addition of treatment or management of another condition, can be seamlessly integrated into the expert system by defining the new function entity and its objectives. Subsequently, a set of rules and facts are incorporated as needed, without disrupting other objectives or functions.
